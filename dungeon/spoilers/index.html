The Adventurer's Companion
to the Great Underground Empire

A two-volume guide to Dungeon — the 616-point mainframe original of Zork

This is a spoilers guide for the game of Dungeon — the 1977–79 MIT original from which Infocom's Zork I, II, and III were later carved. It is presented in two volumes, in the tradition of Infocom's InvisiClues hint books.

Read them in order if you are stuck, in reverse if you are curious, or not at all if you would rather figure everything out yourself — which, honestly, is the most fun way to play.

Volume I — Whispers from the Deep

A hint book. Gentle pokes, structural explanations, and partial spoilers in the classic InvisiClues tiered style. Each puzzle is presented as a question with hints at three increasing levels of detail; you read one, try again, and come back only if you need the next. Also covers the game's history, the parser, the thief, and the general craft of adventuring.

Safe to browse. Won't spoil the game outright unless you deliberately read to the third hint level.

Volume II — The Complete Walkthrough

A complete 616-point walkthrough, spelled out act by act. Every command, every room, every treasure in the order a perfect run executes them. Ends with appendices: full treasure list, master map, magic words, death scenarios, and the location of the last point.

Spoils everything. Do not read unless you are finished or truly stuck.

The dungeon is listening, but by this point it knows who you are.
